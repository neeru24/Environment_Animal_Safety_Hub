Climate Change Basics

Understanding these three simple concepts is the key to understanding climate change.

The Greenhouse Effect

Earth's natural blanket that keeps us warm. But we're making it too thick!

  • Like a car parked in the sun
  • Natural gases trap heat
  • Essential for life on Earth
  • Human activities intensify it
  • Main gas: Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

Simple Analogy: Imagine wearing too many blankets on a warm night.

Global Warming

The Earth's average temperature is rising – and it's happening faster than ever.

  • 1.1°C warmer since 1880
  • Oceans absorbing most heat
  • Polar regions warming fastest
  • More extreme weather events
  • Rising sea levels

Simple Analogy: Like a fever for our planet – small change, big effects.

Human Activities

What we're doing that's changing the climate (and what we can do differently).

  • Burning fossil fuels
  • Deforestation
  • Industrial agriculture
  • Transportation emissions
  • Waste production

Simple Analogy: Like smoking – we know it's harmful, but change is hard.

Climate Change Timeline

How we got here and where we're headed if we don't take action.

1850s

The Industrial Revolution

Widespread use of coal begins. Scientists first theorize that CO₂ could trap heat in the atmosphere.

Key Fact: Atmospheric CO₂ was about 280 parts per million.

1950s

The Great Acceleration

Post-war boom leads to massive increase in fossil fuel use, deforestation, and industrial activities.

Key Fact: Scientists begin measuring rising CO₂ levels at Mauna Loa observatory.

1980s

Climate Science Matures

Clear evidence emerges of human-caused climate change. First international climate conferences held.

Key Fact: 1988 was the hottest year on record at the time.

2020s

Climate Emergency

Record-breaking temperatures, extreme weather events, and scientific consensus on urgent action needed.

Key Fact: CO₂ levels now exceed 420 ppm – highest in 4 million years.

2050

Two Possible Futures

With action: Net-zero emissions, stable climate. Without action: 2°C+ warming, severe impacts.

Key Fact: The choices we make today determine which future we get.

What Climate Change Looks Like

These are the real-world effects we're already experiencing.

Rising Sea Levels

Melting ice and expanding warm water threaten coastal communities worldwide.

Extreme Weather

More intense hurricanes, floods, droughts, and heatwaves becoming common.

Ecosystem Collapse

Coral bleaching, forest fires, and species extinction accelerating.

Food Security

Crop failures and fisheries disruption threatening global food supplies.

Solutions That Actually Work

The good news: We have the technology and knowledge to solve this. Here's how.

Clean Energy Transition

Switching to solar, wind, and other renewables is now cheaper than fossil fuels in most places.

Sustainable Transportation

Electric vehicles, public transit, biking, and walking reduce emissions and improve health.

Nature-Based Solutions

Protecting forests, restoring wetlands, and regenerative agriculture absorb carbon naturally.

Circular Economy

Redesigning systems to eliminate waste and keep materials in use reduces emissions dramatically.

Myths vs Facts

Clearing up common misunderstandings about climate change.

MYTH: "Climate has always changed naturally"

While true, current changes are 10-100x faster than natural cycles, and perfectly match human CO₂ emissions.

FACT: Human activities are driving unprecedented rapid change
MYTH: "It's too expensive to fix"

The cost of inaction is far higher. Every $1 spent on prevention saves $7 in future damages.

FACT: Climate action creates jobs and saves money long-term
MYTH: "One person can't make a difference"

Individual actions create social norms, influence markets, and pressure governments to act.

FACT: Collective individual action drives systemic change
